Buy and Sell Business in Singapore – Contractor and Property Services Companies

Buying or selling a contractor business in Singapore — particularly in fire protection, waterproofing, M&E or property maintenance — involves considerations that are specific to the regulated nature of the industry. Licences, certifications, project track records and key personnel are often the most valuable assets in a contractor business acquisition.

What Makes a Contractor Business Valuable to a Buyer

  • Contractor licences and registrations — BCA contractor registration, SCDF fire protection contractor registration and HDB RCR registration take time to obtain. An established company with these in place has a head start over a new entrant.
  • Ongoing contracts and repeat clients — a portfolio of MCST maintenance contracts, long-term service agreements or recurring government project work provides stable revenue.
  • Project track record — completed projects across fire sprinkler, fire alarm, waterproofing and M&E works demonstrate credibility and support future tender bids.
  • Skilled workforce — trained technicians, licensed electricians and experienced site supervisors are difficult to recruit and retain.
  • Equipment and tools — injection pumps, pipe threading equipment, testing instruments and site tools have replacement value.

Key Due Diligence Points When Buying a Contractor Business

  • Verify all licences and registrations are current and transferable
  • Review outstanding project liabilities and warranty obligations
  • Confirm work pass status and employment contracts for key workers
  • Check for outstanding safety violations, MOM notices or insurance claims
  • Assess debtor and creditor positions — construction businesses often have significant outstanding receivables
  • Understand which contracts are tied to key individuals rather than the company entity

Selling a Contractor Business

When selling a fire protection or M&E contracting business, the key to achieving a good valuation is demonstrating recurring revenue, clean compliance records and a skilled team that will remain with the business post-sale. Buyers in this sector typically pay a premium for businesses with strong relationships with building owners, MCSTs or government agencies.
