Can a Foreigner Register a Company in Singapore – Contractor Business Setup

Yes — foreigners can register a company in Singapore. Singapore is one of the most open business environments in the world for foreign company incorporation. A foreigner can be a shareholder and director of a Singapore private limited company. However, there are specific additional requirements for setting up a contractor business in fire protection, M&E or construction.

Basic Company Registration Requirements

  • At least one director who is ordinarily resident in Singapore — this can be a Singapore citizen, PR or Employment Pass holder. A foreigner who is not resident in Singapore must appoint a local resident director.
  • A registered Singapore business address
  • At least one shareholder (can be the foreign owner)
  • A company secretary appointed within six months of incorporation
  • Registration through ACRA via the Bizfile+ portal

Additional Requirements for Contractor Businesses

Registering the company is only the first step. To legally carry out fire protection, M&E or construction works in Singapore, the company must also obtain relevant contractor licences:

  • BCA contractor registration — required for general building and civil engineering works. Different registration grades are available depending on project value.
  • SCDF fire protection contractor registration — required to install and maintain fire sprinkler systems, fire alarm systems and other fire protection systems.
  • HDB Renovation Contractor Registration — required to carry out renovation works in HDB flats.
  • BizSAFE certification — required for most construction and M&E worksites in Singapore.

Work Pass Considerations for Foreign Owners

A foreign owner who wishes to work in and manage the business in Singapore needs an Employment Pass (EP). EP eligibility depends on the applicant's qualifications, the company's business activities and MOM's assessment. Simply being a shareholder does not automatically grant the right to work in Singapore.
