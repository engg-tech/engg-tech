Ceiling Leak Repair Singapore – Contractor Selection, Cost and What to Expect

Ceiling leak repair in Singapore involves more than just patching the stain on the ceiling. A proper repair addresses the source of the leak — not just the symptom. Choosing the right contractor and understanding what the job involves helps ensure the repair lasts.

Choosing the Right Contractor for Ceiling Leak Repair

Not all contractors handle ceiling leaks the same way. The right contractor depends on the confirmed source of the leak:

  • Waterproofing contractor — needed when the leak is caused by a failed waterproofing membrane in the bathroom or toilet above. They will assess the floor above, hack tiles if necessary and apply a new waterproofing layer.
  • PU grouting specialist — appropriate when the leak is caused by a concrete slab crack. They inject polyurethane resin to seal the crack internally without hacking.
  • Plumber — needed when the source is a burst or corroded pipe. The pipe section must be accessed and replaced.
  • General contractor — once the source is fixed, a general contractor repairs the ceiling finishes — patching plaster, repainting or replacing the false ceiling if needed.

Engaging a contractor who can diagnose the source before quoting is important. A quote given without an inspection may not address the actual problem.

Factors That Affect Ceiling Leak Repair Cost in Singapore

  • Source of the leak — toilet waterproofing repair involving hacking and retiling is significantly more costly than PU injection grouting for a slab crack
  • Access to the unit above — if the unit above is unoccupied, tenanted or owner-occupied, accessing it for inspection and repair can affect the timeline and logistics
  • Extent of ceiling damage below — minor staining requires repainting; significant plaster damage or a collapsed false ceiling requires more extensive repair
  • Number of affected areas — a single drip point is simpler than widespread seepage across a large area of ceiling

What to Expect During and After the Repair

If the repair involves waterproofing the floor above, the toilet or bathroom will be out of use for several days during hacking, waterproofing and retiling works. After completion, the area below should be monitored for two to four weeks — especially during heavy rain or active use of the bathroom above — to confirm the leak has fully stopped before repainting or restoring the ceiling.

For PU injection works, the affected area can typically be used the same day. Monitor for recurrence over the following weeks before proceeding with cosmetic ceiling repairs.
