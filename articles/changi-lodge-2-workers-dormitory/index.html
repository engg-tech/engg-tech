Changi Lodge 2 Workers Dormitory – Fire Protection and Building Maintenance

Changi Lodge 2 is a workers dormitory in the Changi area of East Singapore, providing accommodation for foreign workers in the construction and marine sectors. Workers dormitory facilities accommodate large numbers of residents and are classified as large-scale residential buildings — making fire protection compliance, waterproofing maintenance and building safety critical obligations.

Fire Protection Requirements for Workers Dormitories

Workers dormitories in Singapore are required to comply with Singapore's Fire Safety Act and the Foreign Employee Dormitories Act (FEDA). Fire protection systems are a mandatory requirement and must be regularly inspected and maintained. Common fire protection systems in dormitory facilities include:

  • Fire sprinkler systems (SPK) throughout dormitory blocks and common areas
  • Fire alarm systems (FA) with detection and alert coverage across all zones
  • Dry riser (DR) and hose reel (HR) systems for firefighting access
  • Fire hydrant (FH) systems for external firefighting
  • Emergency lighting and exit signage
  • Fire extinguisher supply and maintenance

Building Waterproofing and Maintenance

Dormitory blocks with high occupancy experience accelerated wear on bathrooms, toilets and common wet areas. Common waterproofing and maintenance works include:

  • Toilet and bathroom waterproofing membrane replacement
  • PU injection grouting for ceiling leaks and concrete slab cracks
  • Tile and grout repair in communal bathrooms
  • Roof and external wall waterproofing
  • Plumbing repair and drain choke clearing

Electrical and M&E Maintenance

  • Distribution board inspection and maintenance
  • Lighting replacement and electrical troubleshooting
  • Air conditioning maintenance and servicing coordination
  • Emergency power system testing
