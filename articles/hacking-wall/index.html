Hacking Wall – Renovation Context and Sequence of Works

Wall hacking is almost always one of the first physical works to happen on a renovation site. Understanding when it is needed, how it fits into the broader renovation sequence and what needs to be prepared beforehand helps homeowners and project managers avoid delays and cost overruns.

When Is Wall Hacking Needed in a Renovation?

  • Open-concept conversion — removing a wall between the living room and kitchen or between two bedrooms to create a larger combined space
  • Bathroom or toilet enlargement — expanding a wet area by removing the boundary wall and reconfiguring the layout
  • Doorway creation or relocation — hacking an opening through an existing wall to create a new doorway or enlarge an existing one
  • Retiling works — hacking existing tiles and screed before laying new flooring or wall tiles
  • Plumbing and electrical chasing — hacking channels into walls to run new pipes or cables concealed within the wall

Where Wall Hacking Fits in the Renovation Sequence

Hacking is typically the first trade on site after protection and site preparation. The general sequence is:

  1. Protect existing finishes and neighbours from dust and debris
  2. Hacking and demolition works
  3. Structural or waterproofing works if required
  4. Mechanical and electrical rough-in
  5. Plastering, screeding and surface preparation
  6. Tiling and finishing works
  7. Carpentry and fittings installation
  8. Painting and final touch-ups

Starting hacking before permits are in place or before M&E works are marked out can cause problems — for example, accidentally damaging concealed pipes or cables during demolition.

What to Prepare Before Hacking Starts

  • Obtain renovation permit if required (HDB, condo MCST)
  • Identify and mark all concealed water pipes, electrical conduits and gas lines in the walls to be hacked
  • Inform neighbours and building management of the hacking schedule — most buildings restrict hacking to specific hours
  • Arrange adequate dust containment — hacking generates significant dust that can spread throughout the unit and affect neighbours
  • Confirm debris disposal plan — hacking waste must be removed promptly and disposed of at an approved facility
