Memorandum and Articles of Association Singapore – What It Is and Why It Matters

The Memorandum and Articles of Association (M&AA) is a foundational document in Singapore company law. It defines a company's constitution — its name, business activities, shareholder rights, director powers and internal management rules. Understanding it is relevant for anyone setting up a contractor business or construction company in Singapore.

What Is the Memorandum and Articles of Association?

Under the Singapore Companies Act, the Memorandum and Articles of Association historically served as two separate documents — the Memorandum defined the company's external relationship with the world (its name, registered office and business objects), while the Articles governed internal operations (meetings, voting, director appointments and share transfers).

Since the Companies Act was amended, Singapore companies now adopt a single Constitution document that combines both functions. However, older companies may still have their original M&AA on file with ACRA (Accounting and Corporate Regulatory Authority).

Why It Matters for Contractor and Construction Businesses

When setting up a fire protection, waterproofing or M&E contracting business in Singapore, the company constitution needs to accurately reflect the business activities. This matters because:

  • ACRA registration requires a description of business activities — this should correctly list fire protection works, M&E contracting, waterproofing services or other applicable activities
  • Licensing applications with BCA (Building and Construction Authority) for contractor registration require the company to demonstrate it is properly incorporated for the relevant works
  • HDB Renovation Contractor Registration (RCR) applications require the company to be properly incorporated with relevant business activities stated
  • Government tender applications and project awards require bidding companies to be properly registered with appropriate business activity codes

Business Activity Codes for Contractors in Singapore

When registering a contractor business in Singapore, the relevant Singapore Standard Industrial Classification (SSIC) codes include fire protection engineering, general building construction, electrical installation, plumbing and waterproofing works. Selecting the correct codes ensures the company can legally carry out and tender for the relevant works.
