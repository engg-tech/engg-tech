Pearl Grass Singapore – Landscaping and Turf Maintenance

Pearl grass (Hemarthria compressa) is a fine-leafed, low-growing turf grass commonly used in Singapore for residential gardens, condominium landscaping, roadside verges and decorative ground cover. It is well-suited to Singapore's tropical climate — heat-tolerant, relatively low-maintenance and capable of forming a dense, even turf cover.

Where Pearl Grass Is Used in Singapore

  • Landed property gardens — a popular choice for front and back lawns in landed homes, particularly for owners who want a tidy, low-growing turf without frequent mowing
  • Condominium landscaping — used in common areas, garden pathways and decorative lawn sections within condominium developments
  • Commercial and industrial landscaping — planted along building perimeters, carpark borders and open green spaces in commercial and industrial estates
  • Roadside and park landscaping — used by NParks and town councils for low-maintenance green coverage along road verges and public spaces

How to Maintain Pearl Grass in Singapore

  • Mowing — pearl grass grows slowly and requires less frequent mowing than other turf types. Mow to a height of 3 to 5 cm to maintain a neat appearance.
  • Watering — established pearl grass is relatively drought-tolerant. Water deeply once or twice a week rather than shallow daily watering to encourage deep root growth.
  • Fertilising — apply a balanced slow-release fertiliser two to three times a year to maintain healthy growth and colour.
  • Weed control — pearl grass can be overtaken by weeds if not properly maintained. Manual weeding or selective herbicide application helps keep the turf dense and weed-free.
  • Aeration — for compacted soil areas, occasional aeration improves drainage and root development.

Pearl Grass vs Other Common Singapore Turf Types

  • Pearl grass vs Cow grass (Axonopus compressus) — cow grass is coarser and more durable under heavy foot traffic. Pearl grass has a finer texture and neater appearance but is less suitable for high-traffic areas.
  • Pearl grass vs Zoysia grass — Zoysia is denser and more wear-resistant but slower to establish. Pearl grass establishes faster and is easier to maintain at lower cost.
