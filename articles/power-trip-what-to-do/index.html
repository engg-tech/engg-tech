Power Trip What To Do

Power trip issues are commonly caused by overloaded electrical circuits, faulty appliances, damaged wiring or circuit breaker faults. When a power trip occurs, proper troubleshooting steps can help identify the issue and reduce the risk of further electrical problems.

What To Do During a Power Trip

  • Check whether neighbouring units also lost power
  • Inspect the circuit breaker or RCCB panel
  • Switch off high-power appliances temporarily
  • Reset the tripped breaker carefully if safe to do so
  • Monitor for repeated electrical trips or burning smells

Common Causes of Power Trips

  • Overloaded electrical circuits
  • Faulty household appliances
  • Damaged electrical wiring
  • Water leakage affecting power points
  • Defective circuit breakers or RCCB units

Signs That Electrical Inspection May Be Needed

  • Repeated circuit breaker trips
  • Sparking sockets or switches
  • Burning smells from electrical points
  • Flickering lights or unstable power supply
  • Power trips after using specific appliances

Benefits of Proper Electrical Maintenance

  • Improved electrical safety
  • Reduced recurring power interruptions
  • Better reliability for electrical systems
  • Reduced risk of appliance damage
  • Improved long-term operational stability

Electrical systems may deteriorate over time due to ageing infrastructure, overloaded circuits, moisture exposure or damaged components. Routine inspection and timely troubleshooting can help improve electrical reliability and safety.
