S Pass vs E Pass Singapore – Work Passes for Construction and M&E Employers

For construction, fire protection and M&E companies in Singapore that need to hire foreign workers, understanding the difference between an S Pass and an Employment Pass (E Pass) is important. The two passes serve different employee levels and come with different employer obligations, quota requirements and salary thresholds.

S Pass – For Mid-Level Skilled Workers

The S Pass is designed for mid-level skilled foreign employees. In the construction and M&E sector, it is commonly used for:

  • Experienced fire protection technicians and supervisors
  • Skilled electrical technicians and M&E coordinators
  • Experienced waterproofing specialists and site supervisors
  • Technical staff with relevant trade qualifications

Key S Pass considerations for construction employers:

  • Subject to a foreign worker quota — the number of S Pass holders a company can employ depends on its ratio of local employees
  • Subject to a monthly levy payable by the employer
  • Minimum salary threshold applies — check MOM's current requirements as these are updated periodically
  • Employer must contribute CPF for S Pass holders

Employment Pass – For Professional and Managerial Roles

The Employment Pass is for professional, managerial and executive roles. In a contractor business, this is typically used for:

  • Project managers and senior engineers
  • Company directors and business owners working in Singapore
  • Senior M&E consultants and technical specialists

Key Employment Pass considerations:

  • Not subject to foreign worker quota — no cap on EP holders relative to local headcount
  • No foreign worker levy
  • Higher minimum salary threshold than S Pass — check MOM's current COMPASS framework requirements
  • Employer must contribute CPF for EP holders

Work Permits for Construction Site Workers

Note that for general construction site workers — labourers, pipe fitters, helpers — the relevant pass is a Work Permit, not an S Pass or EP. Work Permits have different quota rules, source country restrictions and levy structures specific to the construction sector.
