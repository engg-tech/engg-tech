Singapore Grass – Types, Uses and Turf Installation

Singapore's tropical climate — hot, humid and rainy year-round — means not all grass types perform equally well here. Choosing the right grass for your residential garden, condominium landscape or commercial green space affects maintenance requirements, durability and long-term appearance.

Common Grass Types Used in Singapore

  • Cow Grass (Axonopus compressus) — the most widely used turf grass in Singapore. Coarse-textured, fast-growing and durable. Handles moderate foot traffic well. Common in HDB estates, parks and roadsides. Requires regular mowing. Low cost.
  • Pearl Grass (Hemarthria compressa) — fine-textured, low-growing and neat in appearance. Slower-growing than cow grass so requires less frequent mowing. Suitable for residential gardens and decorative landscaping where appearance is prioritised over heavy use.
  • Zoysia Grass — dense, wear-resistant and tolerant of partial shade. Forms a tight, carpet-like turf. Good for areas with moderate foot traffic. Slower to establish but long-lasting once settled.
  • Manila Grass (Zoysia matrella) — a finer-bladed Zoysia variety used in golf courses, sports fields and high-end residential landscaping. More expensive and requires more care than standard cow or pearl grass.

Turf Installation Process

  1. Site assessment — evaluate soil condition, drainage, sunlight exposure and existing vegetation
  2. Ground preparation — clear existing vegetation, level the ground and improve soil drainage if needed
  3. Topsoil application — apply topsoil or compost to improve soil quality and support grass establishment
  4. Turf laying — roll turf is laid in staggered rows, pressed firmly to remove air pockets and ensure root contact with the soil
  5. Watering and establishment — newly laid turf needs frequent watering for the first two to four weeks while roots establish

Turf Replacement

Existing turf that is patchy, weed-infested or heavily compacted can be replaced. The old turf is removed, the ground is prepared and new turf is laid. This is commonly done for residential gardens, condominium common areas and commercial building surrounds.
