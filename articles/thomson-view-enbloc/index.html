Thomson View Enbloc Singapore – Reinstatement and Renovation Services

Thomson View is a private condominium in the Upper Thomson area of Singapore that has been subject to enbloc sale discussions. When a development undergoes a successful enbloc sale, all owners are required to vacate and hand over their units within a specified period. This triggers a range of reinstatement and renovation requirements for affected residents.

What Happens to Residents After an Enbloc Sale

When an enbloc sale is completed, all unit owners receive their sale proceeds and must vacate the development within the agreed timeline. Owners who have tenants in their units must manage tenancy termination and any reinstatement obligations under their respective tenancy agreements. Residents then typically need to find new accommodation and carry out renovation works in their new homes.

Reinstatement Obligations for Enbloc Residents

If you are a tenant in a unit affected by an enbloc sale, your tenancy may be terminated early. Depending on your lease agreement, you may be required to reinstate the unit before handing it back to the owner. Reinstatement works typically include:

  • Removal of tenant-installed fixtures, partitions and furniture fittings
  • Wall patching and repainting
  • Flooring restoration if changes were made during the tenancy
  • Restoration of electrical points and M&E modifications

Renovation for Your New Home

Residents moving into a new property after an enbloc typically need renovation works in their new unit. Common requirements include:

  • Bathroom waterproofing and retiling for resale units
  • Kitchen renovation and carpentry
  • Electrical works and additional power points
  • Painting and flooring installation
  • Fire protection system checks for the new development
