Ceiling Leak – Causes, Signs and Repair Methods

A ceiling leak is one of the most common property problems in Singapore. Because most residential and commercial buildings are multi-storey, a leak from one unit directly affects the unit below. Identifying the true cause of a ceiling leak is the most important step — the wrong repair method applied to the wrong source wastes time and money.

Common Causes of Ceiling Leaks

  • Toilet or bathroom floor leakage from the unit above — the most common cause. Worn or failed waterproofing membrane under the toilet floor allows water to seep through the slab into the ceiling below.
  • Pipe leakage — a burst, cracked or corroded pipe within the floor slab or above the false ceiling releases water that tracks along the slab and drips down.
  • Air-conditioning condensate overflow — a blocked or overflowing AC drip tray causes water to drip from the ceiling, often concentrated below the indoor AC unit.
  • Roof or external wall seepage — for top-floor units, water can enter through a deteriorated roof membrane, cracked parapet or failed external wall joint.
  • Concrete slab cracks — structural cracks in the floor slab, particularly in older buildings, allow water to pass through directly.

Warning Signs of a Ceiling Leak

  • Damp patch or water stain on the ceiling
  • Paint bubbling, peeling or blistering on the ceiling surface
  • Water dripping directly from the ceiling
  • Mould or mildew forming on the ceiling in a localised area
  • Musty smell in the room below a bathroom

Repair Methods Depending on the Cause

  • Toilet waterproofing above — requires hacking the floor tiles in the unit above, applying a new waterproofing membrane and retiling. This is the only permanent solution for a failed bathroom membrane.
  • PU injection grouting — used when the leak is caused by a concrete slab crack or joint. PU resin is injected into the crack to seal it from within without hacking.
  • Pipe replacement — if a burst pipe is confirmed, the affected section is replaced. Some pipes are accessible above false ceilings; others are embedded in the slab and require careful hacking to access.
  • AC drip tray clearing — blocked condensate drainage is resolved by clearing the tray and outlet pipe, a relatively simple maintenance task.
