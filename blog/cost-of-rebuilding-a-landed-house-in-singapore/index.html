Cost Of Rebuilding A Landed House In Singapore – Rebuild Guide

Cost of rebuilding a landed house in Singapore depends on factors such as property type, built-up area, structural complexity, architectural design, material selection and construction requirements.

Engg-Tech provides professional landed house rebuilding, reconstruction and A&A works support for residential properties in Singapore.

Landed homeowners may choose rebuilding instead of renovation when the property structure is aging, layouts are outdated or major redesign and expansion works are required.

Estimated Rebuild Cost Ranges

  • Terrace house rebuild: approximately S$650,000 to S$1,500,000+
  • Semi-detached rebuild: approximately S$900,000 to S$2,000,000+
  • Detached bungalow rebuild: approximately S$1,600,000 to S$3,800,000+

Construction costs are commonly calculated based on built-up area and may range around S$300 to S$700+ per square foot depending on design complexity and finish quality.

Common Cost Components

  • Demolition and debris removal
  • Foundation and structural works
  • Architectural and design services
  • Mechanical and electrical works
  • Interior finishing and carpentry
  • Authority submissions and approvals

Professional fees for architects, engineers and authority submissions can form a significant portion of the total rebuild budget.

Factors Affecting Rebuild Cost

  • Land size and built-up area
  • Number of storeys and attic levels
  • Basement or swimming pool construction
  • Soil condition and piling requirements
  • Material and finish selection
  • URA and BCA compliance requirements

Older landed houses may also require additional structural strengthening, foundation upgrading and waterproofing rectification before reconstruction works proceed.

Rebuild vs A&A Works

Some homeowners may choose A&A works instead of full rebuilding when the existing structure remains usable and only layout modifications or upgrading works are needed.

  • A&A works are generally lower cost
  • Full rebuild offers greater design flexibility
  • Reconstruction may retain portions of existing structure
  • Approval requirements vary depending on project scope

Reconstruction and new erection projects usually involve more extensive authority submissions and longer construction timelines.

Typical Rebuild Timeline

  • Design and planning phase
  • URA and BCA submissions
  • Demolition and site clearance
  • Structural construction works
  • M&E and interior finishing
  • Final inspection and handover

A full landed house rebuild project may take approximately 18 to 30 months depending on design complexity and approval timelines.
