Door Varnish Singapore – Wooden Door Refinishing and Restoration

Door varnish is the process of sanding, treating and applying a protective varnish coating to a wooden door to restore its appearance and protect it from moisture, scratches and daily wear. In Singapore's humid climate, wooden doors deteriorate faster than in drier environments — the combination of heat, humidity and frequent use causes varnish to peel, fade and crack over time.

Refinishing a door with fresh varnish restores the natural grain and colour of the wood, extends its lifespan and protects it against further moisture damage. It is a cost-effective alternative to full door replacement.

Signs Your Door Needs Varnishing

  • Peeling, flaking or bubbling varnish on the door surface
  • Faded or uneven colour due to sun exposure
  • Surface cracks or grain opening caused by moisture absorption
  • Dull or worn finish that no longer protects the wood
  • Swelling or warping at the edges due to moisture ingress

The Door Varnishing Process

  1. Sanding — the existing varnish is sanded down to remove peeling layers and open the wood grain for better adhesion
  2. Surface repair — cracks, dents and imperfections are filled and smoothed
  3. Priming — a wood primer or sealer is applied to prepare the surface
  4. Varnish application — two to three coats of varnish are applied, with light sanding between coats for a smooth finish
  5. Final inspection — the finish is checked for consistency and evenness

Types of Varnish Finish

  • Gloss — high shine finish, formal appearance, shows reflections
  • Semi-gloss — moderate sheen, most commonly used for HDB and condo doors
  • Matte — low shine, natural wood look, less visible surface imperfections

Types of Doors Suitable for Varnishing

  • HDB main doors and bedroom doors
  • Solid timber doors in landed properties
  • Veneer panel doors in condominiums
  • Timber wardrobe and cabinet doors
