Epoxy Grout for Shower Singapore – Mould Prevention and Waterproofing

Shower areas are the most demanding environment for tile grout. They are wet multiple times daily, rarely fully dry in Singapore's humid climate and are exposed to soap, shampoo and cleaning chemicals. Standard cement grout in shower areas typically develops black mould within months and begins to crack and deteriorate within a few years. Epoxy grout is specifically suited to resolve these problems.

Why Shower Areas Need Epoxy Grout

  • Constant moisture exposure — shower walls and floors are wet daily. Cement grout absorbs this moisture, creating the ideal conditions for mould growth. Epoxy grout is non-porous and does not absorb water.
  • Soap and chemical resistance — shower products, descalers and bathroom cleaners degrade cement grout over time. Epoxy grout is chemically resistant and unaffected by these products.
  • Singapore's humidity — even when not in use, Singapore's high ambient humidity means shower areas rarely fully dry. Cement grout in these conditions deteriorates faster than in drier climates.

How Epoxy Grout Prevents Mould in Showers

Mould in shower grout grows because moisture is trapped within the porous cement matrix. Epoxy grout eliminates this pathway — water cannot penetrate the joint, so mould has no moisture source to feed on. The result is a shower that stays cleaner for significantly longer and requires less aggressive cleaning to maintain.

Shower Floor vs Shower Wall Grout

Both shower floor and wall grout benefit from epoxy, but the shower floor is particularly important. Water pools on the floor before draining, and grout joints that are not fully waterproof allow water to penetrate the screed beneath the tiles — eventually causing leakage to the unit below. Epoxy grout on the shower floor provides a watertight joint that prevents this.

What to Expect During a Shower Epoxy Grouting Job

The existing cement grout is raked out to a sufficient depth using an oscillating multi-tool or grout saw. The joints are cleaned and dried as much as possible before epoxy grout is applied. The shower should not be used for at least 24 hours after application to allow the epoxy to cure fully.
