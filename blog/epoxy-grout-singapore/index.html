Epoxy Grout Singapore – What It Is and How It Differs from Cement Grout

Epoxy grout is a tile jointing material made from epoxy resin and a hardener, as opposed to standard cement grout which is made from Portland cement and sand. The difference in composition results in significantly different performance characteristics — particularly in wet, high-traffic and hygiene-sensitive environments.

How Epoxy Grout Differs from Cement Grout

  • Water resistance — epoxy grout is non-porous and does not absorb water. Cement grout is porous and can allow moisture to penetrate behind tiles over time.
  • Stain resistance — epoxy grout resists oil, grease, cleaning chemicals and food stains. Cement grout stains easily and is difficult to clean once discoloured.
  • Mould and bacteria — because epoxy grout does not absorb moisture, mould and mildew cannot grow in the joint. Cement grout in wet areas frequently develops black mould over time.
  • Durability — epoxy grout is harder and more resistant to cracking and shrinkage. Cement grout can crack over time, especially with structural movement or temperature changes.
  • Colour stability — epoxy grout holds its colour over many years. Cement grout fades, discolours and becomes uneven with age and cleaning.

Types of Epoxy Grout

  • Two-component epoxy grout — resin and hardener mixed on site, most commonly used for floor and wall tiles in residential and commercial applications
  • Epoxy grout with marble dust or quartz — used for decorative joints where a specific texture or finish is desired
  • Flexible epoxy grout — used in areas with slight structural movement, such as large format tile installations

Where Epoxy Grout Is Typically Used in Singapore

  • Toilet and bathroom floor and wall tiles
  • Kitchen splashbacks and countertops
  • Commercial kitchen and food preparation areas
  • Swimming pool surrounds and wet deck areas
  • Hospital and clinic floors
  • Any tiled area requiring high hygiene standards
