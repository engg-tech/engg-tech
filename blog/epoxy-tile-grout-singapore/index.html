Epoxy Tile Grout Singapore – Floor and Wall Tile Grout Replacement

Over time, tile grout in Singapore homes and commercial spaces deteriorates — it cracks, discolours, develops mould and in some cases allows water to seep behind tiles. Replacing old cement grout with epoxy tile grout resolves these issues and provides a long-lasting, low-maintenance finish.

Signs Your Tile Grout Needs Replacing

  • Grout lines are black, brown or permanently stained despite cleaning
  • Grout is cracked, crumbling or missing in sections
  • Tiles feel hollow or loose — a sign moisture has penetrated behind them
  • Water is seeping through grout lines to the unit below
  • Mould keeps returning in the grout lines within weeks of cleaning

Floor Tile Grout vs Wall Tile Grout

Both floor and wall tiles benefit from epoxy grout replacement, but the process differs slightly:

  • Floor tiles — old grout is raked out using an oscillating tool or grout saw. The joints are cleaned and epoxy grout is applied and pressed into the joints. Excess is cleaned off before it cures. Floor grout takes more wear and benefits greatly from epoxy's hardness.
  • Wall tiles — the process is similar but requires more care to prevent dripping on lower tiles during application. Wall grout is exposed to splashing and humidity rather than foot traffic, making epoxy's moisture resistance the key benefit.

Can Epoxy Grout Be Applied Over Existing Grout?

In most cases, existing grout should be raked out to a minimum depth before applying epoxy grout. Applying epoxy over intact cement grout does not bond well and will peel or crack over time. A thorough removal ensures proper adhesion and a durable result.

How Long Does Epoxy Tile Grouting Take?

A typical toilet or bathroom takes one to two days — one day for raking and preparation, and one day for epoxy application and curing. Larger areas such as kitchen floors or living room tiles take longer depending on the surface area and number of joints involved.
