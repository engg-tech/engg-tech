HDB Ceiling Leak – Neighbour Disputes, Responsibility and the HDB Process

A ceiling leak in an HDB flat almost always involves the neighbour living in the unit directly above. This creates a situation that is as much about neighbour relations and dispute resolution as it is about waterproofing repair. Understanding who is responsible and how the HDB process works helps you resolve the issue more efficiently.

Who Is Responsible for an HDB Ceiling Leak?

In most cases, the owner of the unit above is responsible for rectifying the source of the leak — because the leak typically originates from their floor (the ceiling of the unit below). Common sources include:

  • Failed waterproofing under the toilet or bathroom floor above
  • A burst or leaking pipe within the floor slab between units
  • Leaking from the kitchen floor or wet area above

The owner of the unit below is generally responsible for repairing damage to their own ceiling — staining, paint peeling, plaster damage — once the source above has been fixed.

What to Do First

  1. Document the leak — photograph the damp patch, water stains or dripping. Note the date and whether it is continuous or occurs only during certain times (e.g. when the neighbour showers).
  2. Approach the neighbour above — explain the situation calmly and show them your documentation. Most neighbours are willing to cooperate once they understand the problem.
  3. Engage a contractor for a joint inspection — a waterproofing contractor can assess both units to identify the source and recommend the appropriate repair.

When the Neighbour Does Not Cooperate

If the neighbour above refuses to acknowledge the problem or refuses to carry out repairs, HDB can be approached for assistance. HDB's process for inter-floor leakage disputes typically involves:

  • HDB conducting a site inspection to assess the situation
  • HDB mediating between the two parties to reach an agreement on repair responsibility and cost sharing
  • In cases where mediation fails, the matter may be referred to the Community Disputes Management Tribunals (CDMT)

Cost Sharing for HDB Ceiling Leaks

HDB generally recommends that repair costs be shared between the two affected owners — the owner above bears the cost of rectifying the source, and the owner below bears the cost of repairing their ceiling finishes. However, this can vary depending on the specific circumstances and any prior agreements between the parties.
