HDB Resale Renovation Package Singapore – What to Expect and How to Plan

Buying an HDB resale flat means inheriting the previous owner's renovations, wear and tear and sometimes outdated fittings. A renovation package for a resale flat is different from a BTO renovation — you may need to hack existing tiles, remove old fixtures and fix waterproofing issues before the new renovation begins. Understanding what is typically involved helps you plan your budget and timeline more accurately.

What a Resale Renovation Package Typically Covers

  • Hacking works — removing old floor tiles, wall tiles, false ceilings and partitions from the previous owner's renovation
  • Waterproofing — reapplying waterproofing membrane in toilets and wet areas before retiling. This is critical in resale flats where the original membrane may have deteriorated
  • Tiling — new floor and wall tiles for bathrooms, kitchen and common areas
  • Painting — full-flat repainting including walls, ceilings and feature surfaces
  • Carpentry — kitchen cabinets, wardrobes, TV console and storage solutions
  • Electrical works — additional power points, lighting installation and wiring updates
  • Plumbing — toilet bowl replacement, basin, shower fitting and tap replacement

What Affects the Cost of a Resale HDB Renovation

  • Flat size — 3-room, 4-room and 5-room flats have significantly different material and labour costs
  • Condition of the existing renovation — an older flat with more hacking required costs more than a recently renovated one
  • Waterproofing scope — if both bathrooms need full hacking and waterproofing, this adds significant cost
  • Material selections — tile grade, cabinet material and fitting quality all affect the final price
  • Carpentry extent — full built-in carpentry throughout the flat costs more than a targeted approach

What to Prioritise in a Resale Renovation

Waterproofing is the most important item in a resale renovation and should never be skipped to save cost. A failed bathroom waterproofing membrane causes leakage to the unit below — a problem that is far more expensive to fix after tiling is complete. Similarly, checking and upgrading electrical wiring in older flats reduces long-term safety risks.

HDB Renovation Permit

Renovations in HDB flats require a permit from HDB. This must be obtained before work starts. Your renovation contractor registered under HDB's Renovation Contractor Registration scheme will handle this on your behalf.
