Office Reinstatement Contractor Singapore – How to Choose and What It Costs

Choosing the right office reinstatement contractor in Singapore is important. A poorly executed reinstatement can result in landlord disputes, deposit forfeiture and additional rectification costs. Here is what to look for and what affects the cost.

What to Check When Choosing a Contractor

  • Experience with commercial reinstatement — not all renovation contractors handle reinstatement. Look for one with a track record of office, retail or F&B reinstatement specifically.
  • BizSAFE certification — required for worksites in Singapore. Confirms the contractor has a proper safety management system in place.
  • Workmen's compensation insurance — protects you from liability if a worker is injured on site during the works.
  • Ability to handle M&E works — electrical and ACMV reinstatement may require licensed workers. Confirm the contractor can handle these in-house or has approved subcontractors.
  • Clear written quotation — a proper quotation should itemise each scope of work, not just give a lump sum. This helps avoid disputes over what is included.

Questions to Ask Before Signing

  • Have you done reinstatement for this building or landlord before?
  • What is included in the quoted price — does it cover debris disposal?
  • How many workers will be on site and what are the daily working hours?
  • What happens if additional works are required after the landlord's inspection?
  • Can you provide a completion certificate or photographic documentation upon handover?

Factors That Affect Reinstatement Cost in Singapore

  • Size of the space — larger offices cost more in both labour and disposal fees
  • Complexity of fit-out — a heavily partitioned office or restaurant with full kitchen takes longer than an open-plan space
  • Access restrictions — buildings with strict working hour limits or lift usage fees add to costs
  • Extent of M&E works — if significant electrical or ACMV modifications were made, the restoration cost is higher
  • Lead time — urgent reinstatements with tight deadlines may carry a premium

When to Engage a Contractor

Engage your reinstatement contractor at least six to eight weeks before your lease expiry date. This allows enough time for site visits, quotation, permit applications, the works themselves and the landlord's inspection before the official handover date.
