Office Reinstatement – What Gets Removed and Restored

Office reinstatement is the most common type of reinstatement work in Singapore. When a company moves out of a leased office space, they are typically required to remove all fit-out elements installed during the tenancy and return the space to its original shell-and-core or bare condition. Here is a breakdown of what is typically involved.

False Ceiling Removal

Most modern offices have a suspended false ceiling installed above the workstations. During reinstatement, the entire ceiling grid, tiles, light fittings, air-conditioning diffusers and any ceiling-mounted features are dismantled and removed. The original slab soffit is exposed, and any holes or anchor points are patched.

Partition Walls

Office partitions — whether plasterboard, glass, timber or modular — are removed completely. Meeting room walls, manager room enclosures, reception counters and feature walls all fall under this scope. After removal, the floor and ceiling where partitions were fixed are made good.

Flooring

Carpet tiles, vinyl flooring, raised access flooring and timber decking installed by the tenant are stripped out. If the original floor was bare concrete screed, it is left exposed and clean. Adhesive residue from carpet or vinyl is typically scraped or ground off.

Electrical and M&E Works

Additional power points, lighting circuits, data cabling, server room cooling and ACMV modifications are removed or restored to the original configuration. The distribution board is returned to its original state with excess circuits removed. All cabling above the ceiling is taken out.

Signage and Branding

Tenant signage at the main entrance, reception wall, lift lobby and any directional signboards are removed. Any adhesive vinyl graphics or painted branding on walls are cleaned off or painted over.

Pantry and Wet Areas

If a pantry or staff toilet was added or modified during the tenancy, plumbing connections, cabinetry and appliance points are removed and restored. Tiling and waterproofing may need to be addressed depending on the original condition of the space.
