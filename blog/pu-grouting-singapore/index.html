PU Grouting Singapore – Cost, Property Types & When to Call

PU grouting is widely used across Singapore to repair water leakage in HDB flats, condominiums, landed properties and commercial buildings. Given Singapore's high rainfall and humidity, concrete structures are particularly vulnerable to seepage and crack-related leakage — making PU grouting one of the most common waterproofing repair methods used locally.

Property Types Covered

  • HDB flats — ceiling leaks between floors, toilet seepage, common corridor cracks
  • Condominiums — basement car parks, swimming pool surrounds, planter boxes, roof slabs
  • Landed properties — basement walls, retaining walls, flat roofs and internal wet areas
  • Commercial and industrial buildings — underground structures, lift pits, loading bays, water tanks

Cost Factors for PU Grouting in Singapore

PU grouting costs in Singapore vary depending on several factors:

  • Extent and length of cracking or leakage area
  • Accessibility of the affected area
  • Number of injection points required
  • Type of PU material used (hydrophilic vs hydrophobic)
  • Whether hacking or surface preparation is needed beforehand

Smaller localised leaks such as a single crack in a toilet wall are generally more straightforward and cost less than widespread basement seepage requiring multiple injection points across a large area.

When Should You Call a PU Grouting Contractor?

  • Water is actively dripping through a wall, ceiling or floor slab
  • You see damp patches or moisture stains that keep returning after painting
  • Cracks in concrete are letting in water during or after rain
  • A previous surface waterproofing repair has failed
  • Your toilet or wet area is leaking to the unit below

The earlier a leakage is addressed, the less damage it causes. Delaying repair often leads to spalling concrete, rusting reinforcement bars and more costly rectification works.

What to Expect from a Site Assessment

A contractor will first inspect the affected area to identify the source and extent of the leakage. Not every wet patch requires PU grouting — some cases are better resolved through epoxy injection, waterproofing membrane application or drainage correction. A proper assessment ensures the right method is recommended.
