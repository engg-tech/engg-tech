PU Grouting – What It Is and How It Works

PU grouting, short for polyurethane grouting, is a waterproofing method that uses liquid polyurethane resin injected under pressure into cracks, joints and voids inside concrete structures. When the resin contacts moisture, it expands and forms a flexible, watertight foam that blocks the leakage pathway from within.

Unlike surface-applied waterproofing coatings, PU grouting works from the inside out — sealing the source of the leak rather than just covering it. This makes it particularly effective for active water seepage where surface treatments alone would fail.

How Polyurethane Resin Works

The polyurethane material used in PU grouting is a two-component or single-component resin. When injected into a wet or damp crack, it reacts with the moisture present and expands — sometimes up to 20 to 30 times its original volume — filling the void completely and creating a sealed barrier.

The cured foam is flexible, meaning it can accommodate minor structural movement without cracking. This is an advantage over rigid epoxy injection which can fracture if the structure shifts slightly.

Types of PU Grouting Materials

  • Hydrophilic PU foam — reacts aggressively with water, ideal for active leaks and wet conditions
  • Hydrophobic PU foam — repels water, used where a more rigid and durable seal is needed
  • PU resin (non-foaming) — used for structural crack repair where flexibility is required without foam expansion

What PU Grouting Is Used For

  • Sealing active water leaks in concrete walls and slabs
  • Waterproofing basement and underground structures
  • Toilet and wet area seepage repair
  • Lift pit leakage sealing
  • Retaining wall and roof slab crack injection

PU Grouting vs Surface Waterproofing

Surface waterproofing membranes and coatings protect structures from the outside. PU grouting addresses leakage that has already penetrated into the concrete. Both methods serve different purposes and are often used together for a complete waterproofing solution.

If water is already entering through a crack or joint, surface coatings alone will not resolve the problem. PU grouting seals the internal pathway first, and surface treatment can then be applied as a secondary measure.
