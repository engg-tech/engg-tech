PU Injection Grouting – The Step-by-Step Process Explained

PU injection grouting is a systematic process that requires proper technique to be effective. Understanding each step helps property owners know what to expect during the repair and why each stage matters.

Step 1 – Leakage Identification and Mapping

The contractor inspects the affected area to map the full extent of cracking and leakage. This includes identifying entry points, tracing moisture patterns and determining whether the leakage is active (water currently flowing) or inactive (dried staining from a past leak).

Active leaks require immediate attention and may need hydrophilic PU foam which reacts quickly with water. Inactive cracks may be better suited to hydrophobic PU or epoxy injection.

Step 2 – Drilling Injection Holes

Holes are drilled at intervals along the crack or leakage pathway, typically at a 45-degree angle to intersect the crack at mid-depth. The spacing between holes depends on the thickness of the concrete and the nature of the crack.

Step 3 – Installing Injection Packers

Metal or plastic packers are installed into the drilled holes. These packers act as nozzles through which the PU resin is injected under pressure. They create a sealed connection between the injection pump and the internal crack.

Step 4 – Injecting the PU Resin

Using a hand pump or electric pump, PU resin is injected through each packer in sequence. The pressure forces the resin deep into the crack. When it contacts moisture inside the concrete, it expands into foam and fills the void completely.

Injection continues until resin flows out of the adjacent packer, confirming the section is fully filled. The contractor then moves to the next packer.

Step 5 – Removing Packers and Patching

Once injection is complete and the resin has cured, the packers are removed. The drilled holes and any surface cracks are patched with cementitious mortar or epoxy filler, leaving a clean finish.

Step 6 – Post-Repair Inspection

The repaired area is monitored after completion to confirm the leakage has stopped. In some cases, a secondary application may be needed if leakage persists through a different pathway not initially identified.

How Long Does It Take?

A typical PU injection grouting repair for a localised crack takes a few hours. Larger areas involving multiple cracks across a basement or retaining wall may take one to two days. The cured resin achieves its full strength within 24 hours in most conditions.
