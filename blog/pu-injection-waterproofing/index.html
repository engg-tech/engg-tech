PU Injection Waterproofing – How It Compares to Other Methods

There is no single best waterproofing method for every situation. PU injection waterproofing is highly effective in specific conditions, but understanding how it compares to other approaches helps you make the right decision for your property.

PU Injection vs Epoxy Injection

Both PU and epoxy are injected under pressure into cracks, but they behave differently once cured.

  • PU injection produces a flexible foam seal. It works in wet and actively leaking conditions because it reacts with water to expand and seal the crack. Best for stopping active water flow.
  • Epoxy injection produces a rigid, structural bond. It is stronger than PU and can restore the load-bearing capacity of a cracked concrete element. Best for dry or inactive cracks where structural repair is needed.

In practice, PU injection is often used first to stop the active leak, followed by epoxy injection to restore structural integrity once the area is dry.

PU Injection vs Cementitious Waterproofing

  • PU injection targets internal crack pathways and is applied through drilled holes. It does not require surface preparation or hacking in most cases.
  • Cementitious waterproofing is applied as a slurry or coating to the surface. It is effective for new construction or areas where the concrete surface is accessible and in good condition.

Cementitious systems work well for prevention. PU injection is better suited for remedial repair where water is already entering the structure.

PU Injection vs Waterproofing Membrane

  • PU injection seals from the inside and does not require hacking away the floor or wall finish.
  • Waterproofing membrane is installed on the surface or under the screed. For a failed membrane, rectification typically requires hacking, new membrane installation and re-tiling — a more disruptive and costly process.

When hacking is not an option — due to tenancy, aesthetics or cost — PU injection is often the preferred remedial solution.

When PU Injection Waterproofing Is the Best Choice

  • Active water is flowing through a crack or joint
  • Hacking is not feasible or desired
  • Surface coatings have already failed
  • The leakage is internal to the concrete structure
  • A fast turnaround is needed
