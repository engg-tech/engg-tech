Reinstatement Services Singapore – Tenancy Law and Landlord Requirements

In Singapore, commercial tenants are legally and contractually obligated to reinstate their leased premises before vacating. This obligation is embedded in most commercial lease agreements and is enforced through the security deposit mechanism. Understanding your obligations under Singapore tenancy law can help avoid costly disputes at the end of a lease.

What Singapore Lease Agreements Typically Require

Most commercial leases in Singapore include a reinstatement clause specifying that the tenant must return the premises to their original condition — as at the commencement of the tenancy — at the tenant's own cost. The original condition is usually defined by a handover inspection report or a set of as-built drawings provided at the start of the tenancy.

Common landlord requirements include:

  • Removal of all tenant-installed partitions, ceilings and flooring
  • Restoration of original electrical distribution boards and wiring
  • Return of ACMV systems to original layout
  • Repainting of walls to original colour or a neutral white
  • Removal of all signage, branding and applied finishes
  • Completion of works within the notice period before lease expiry

Security Deposit and Reinstatement

Singapore commercial landlords typically hold a security deposit of one to three months rent. If reinstatement is not completed to the landlord's satisfaction, the cost of completing the works is deducted from the deposit. If costs exceed the deposit, the landlord may pursue the tenant for the balance.

To protect your deposit, it is advisable to engage a reinstatement contractor early — ideally two to three months before lease expiry — to allow sufficient time for works to be completed and inspected before the handover date.

HDB and JTC Commercial Spaces

For spaces leased from HDB or JTC, reinstatement requirements may be more specific. JTC in particular has detailed guidelines on approved works and the extent of reinstatement required. Some modifications may require written approval to retain — others must be fully removed regardless. Always refer to your specific lease terms and seek clarification from the landlord or building manager before commencing works.
