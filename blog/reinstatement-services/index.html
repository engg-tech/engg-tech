Reinstatement Services – What It Means and What It Covers

Reinstatement refers to the process of restoring a commercial space back to its original condition before a tenant moved in. It is a contractual obligation in most commercial leases in Singapore and must be completed before the tenant vacates and hands back the keys to the landlord.

Reinstatement is not the same as renovation or general cleaning. It specifically involves undoing all modifications made during the tenancy — removing additions and returning the space to its base condition as specified in the lease agreement.

What Does Reinstatement Cover?

  • Hacking and demolition — removal of partition walls, raised platforms, false floors and any structural additions built during the tenancy
  • False ceiling removal — stripping out suspended ceilings and returning to bare slab or the condition specified in the lease
  • Flooring removal — removing vinyl, carpet, raised flooring or floor finishes installed by the tenant
  • Electrical and M&E reinstatement — removing additional power points, lighting tracks, data cabling and ACMV modifications
  • Plastering and painting — patching walls and repainting to original condition
  • Signage removal — taking down all tenant branding, signboards and applied graphics
  • Debris disposal — full removal and disposal of all materials hacked or dismantled

Why Is Reinstatement Required?

Landlords require reinstatement to ensure the space is returned in a neutral, lettable condition — ready for the next tenant to fit out according to their own requirements. Most commercial leases in Singapore explicitly state the reinstatement obligation and tie the return of the security deposit to satisfactory completion of reinstatement works.

Failing to complete reinstatement can result in the landlord deducting reinstatement costs from the deposit or pursuing the tenant for the difference if costs exceed the deposit amount.
