Reinstatement Work Singapore – Timeline, Process and What to Expect

Reinstatement work follows a specific sequence to ensure efficiency and safety on site. Knowing what to expect at each stage helps tenants plan their move-out timeline and avoid last-minute delays that could result in deposit deductions or holding over charges.

Before Work Starts – What to Prepare

  • Obtain a copy of your lease agreement and identify the reinstatement clause
  • Refer to the original handover report or as-built drawings to confirm what needs to be removed
  • Notify the building management and obtain any necessary work permits or access passes for contractors
  • Clear all furniture, equipment and personal items from the premises before reinstatement begins
  • Confirm utility isolation requirements — some buildings require M&E works to be carried out by approved contractors

Typical Sequence of Reinstatement Works

  1. M&E disconnection — electrical, data, ACMV and plumbing systems are safely isolated and removed or restored to original state
  2. False ceiling removal — suspended ceiling grids, tiles and light fittings are dismantled
  3. Partition and feature wall hacking — non-structural partitions, glass panels and feature walls are removed
  4. Flooring removal — carpet, vinyl, raised flooring or timber decking is stripped out
  5. Patching and plastering — wall and ceiling surfaces are made good after hacking
  6. Painting — walls and ceilings are repainted to the specified colour
  7. Debris disposal — all demolished materials are removed and disposed of properly
  8. Final cleaning — the space is cleaned and prepared for handover inspection

How Long Does Reinstatement Take?

A small office of 500 to 1,000 sq ft with moderate fit-out typically takes three to five working days. Larger spaces or those with complex fit-outs — such as restaurants, retail shops or multi-room offices — may require two to three weeks. Always factor in time for the landlord's inspection and any rectification works before the official handover date.

Common Causes of Delay

  • Late engagement of contractor — ideally engage at least two months before lease expiry
  • Disputes over scope — misalignment between tenant and landlord on what needs to be removed
  • Access restrictions — building management limiting working hours or requiring permits
  • Rectification works — landlord requests additional patching or repainting after initial inspection
